Temporal Adaptation of Pre-trained Foundation Models for Music Structure Analysis

Abstract

Audio-based music structure analysis (MSA) remains a fundamental challenge in the field of MIR. Recent studies have shown promising results by fine-tuning pre-trained music foundation models for structural analysis tasks. However, foundation models are pre-trained with a higher temporal resolution and a shorter audio window, which are not well-suited for MSA. As a result, it leads to inefficiencies and prediction biases. In this paper, we propose a \textit{temporal adaption} method applied to music foundation models for the MSA task, which enables the model to efficiently analyze full song length music audio in a single pass by (1) audio window length extension and (2) low-resolution adaptation. Experiments on the Harmonix Set and RWC-Pop datasets demonstrate that the temporal adaptation method significantly improves the function and boundary prediction accuracy, while maintaining comparable memory usage and inference speed.
